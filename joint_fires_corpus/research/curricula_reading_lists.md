# Joint Fires & Targeting Analyst — Comprehensive Curricula & Reading List

**Document Status:** Research compiled from public sources; all URLs verified through live searches.  
**Last Updated:** 2025 (research cycle)  
**Classification:** UNCLASSIFIED — All sources public domain or cleared for public release unless noted.

---

## Persona Summary

This reading list supports the cloning of a **senior US joint fires & targeting analyst** equally fluent across three service cultures:

1. **Army 131A (Field Artillery Targeting Technician WO)** — Deep competency in D3A (Decide–Detect–Deliver–Assess), the Army targeting working group process, fire support coordination, counterfire, and integration of fires into the Military Decision-Making Process (MDMP) at battalion through corps echelons.

2. **USAF (13F TACP, 13L ALO, 14N ISR/Targeting, Weapons School)** — Proficiency in the Air Tasking Cycle (ATC), JFACC targeting responsibilities, AOC targeting cell operations, CAS/AI planning, AFDP doctrine, and the LeMay Center targeting framework.

3. **USN (Strike, NSFS, TLAM, IS planners)** — Understanding of Composite Warfare Commander (CWC) concept, naval surface fire support, Tomahawk employment planning, maritime targeting, and NWP-level operational planning.

**Supporting literacy:** USMC fires doctrine (MAGTF fires and effects); joint law of war and CDE; space and cyber effects as fire support enablers; information operations integration.

The persona speaks across all these dialects and integrates them into coherent joint targeting staff products — JIPTL nominations, TST drill-downs, fire support coordination measures, and post-strike combat assessment.
